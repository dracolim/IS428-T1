About the tech survey

Currently over 1400 responses, the ongoing 2024 survey aims to measure attitudes towards mental health in the tech workplace, and examine the frequency of mental health disorders among tech workers.

Mental Health in Interview

The third graph (Mental Health in Interview) above shows the % of people in our survey that would/would not bring up mental health with a potential employer.

The results show that 67% of people would not bring up mental health in an interview.

This suggest that mental health may still be something that is frowned upon and employer either do not want to hire people with mental health/do not have resources to deal with mental health

Findings

According to Healthhub Singapore (2023), one of the most important actions that people with mental health disorders should take would be to seek treatment from mental health and healthcare professionals.

Additionally, Healthhub also writes "people with mental health disorders often suffer in silence. They keep their condition to themselves until it is too late. Yet, studies have shown that early treatment can improve the progress of recovery for these people. However, late detection or delaying treatment can cause the condition to worsen." These excerpts show us the importance for a person with mental health disorders to seek treatment.

This shows that mental health may not be something that is talked about which causes even more people to hide their mental health.

From our findings,

as a person is more willing to share about his/ her mental health disorder (1 being least willing and 10 being most willing), that same person would also have a higher chance of seeking the treatment he/ she requires.
